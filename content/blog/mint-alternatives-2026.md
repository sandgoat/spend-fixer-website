When Intuit shut down Mint in early 2024, millions of users were left scrambling for a replacement. Two years later, the dust has settled — and the options have never been better. Here's an honest breakdown of what's worth your time.

## Why Mint Users Are Still Frustrated

The post-Mint landscape promised a lot. Most apps delivered... fine. The problem? They replaced Mint's simplicity with complexity. More dashboards, more categories, more manual work.

If you're still hunting for something that just *works*, you're not alone.

### 2. YNAB (Best for Budgeters)

You Need a Budget is the gold standard if you want a full zero-based budgeting system. It's powerful, has a great community, and genuinely changes how people think about money. It also costs $14.99/month and takes weeks to learn.

**Best for:** People who want to overhaul their entire financial life.

### 3. Copilot (Best for iOS)

Copilot is beautifully designed, AI-powered, and Apple ecosystem exclusive. If you're on iPhone and Mac, it's worth a look. Android users are out of luck.

**Best for:** iPhone users who care about aesthetics.

### 4. Monarch Money

Monarch is a solid Mint replacement with good bank syncing, goals tracking, and shared household budgeting. It's $9.99/month and has been steadily improving.

**Best for:** Couples and families managing money together.

### 5. Empower Personal Dashboard (Free)

Formerly Personal Capital, Empower's free tier gives you net worth tracking and investment analysis. The budgeting features are secondary — they really want to sell you wealth management.

**Best for:** Investors who want a free portfolio tracker.

### 6. Simplifi by Quicken

Quicken's modern app is surprisingly good — clean interface, good spending reports, and solid bank coverage. At $3.99/month, it's one of the cheaper paid options.

**Best for:** People who want Quicken without the old Quicken baggage.

### 7. Tiller Money

Tiller syncs your transactions to Google Sheets or Excel automatically. If you love spreadsheets and want total control, Tiller is exceptional. If you don't love spreadsheets... skip it.

**Best for:** Spreadsheet people.
