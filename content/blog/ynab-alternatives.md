YNAB (You Need a Budget) has a cult following for a reason. The zero-based budgeting method works. But $109/year — plus a learning curve that can take months — puts it out of reach for a lot of people.

Here's what to use instead.

## Who Should Skip YNAB

You're probably better off with a YNAB alternative if:

- You don't want to budget every dollar manually each month
- You just want to *see* your spending, not micromanage it
- $109/year feels steep for a budgeting app
- You've tried YNAB and bounced off the learning curve

### For Full Budgeting: Monarch Money

Monarch is the closest to YNAB in terms of feature depth, but with a gentler learning curve and a more modern interface. At $9.99/month, it's $2 cheaper than YNAB's monthly equivalent.

### For Spreadsheet Lovers: Tiller Money

Tiller auto-syncs your bank data to Google Sheets. You get YNAB-level control with zero vendor lock-in. $79/year, and the community has built hundreds of templates.

### For Free Budget Tracking: Empower

Empower's free tier covers the basics — spending categories, net worth, cash flow. It's not as powerful as YNAB but costs nothing.
